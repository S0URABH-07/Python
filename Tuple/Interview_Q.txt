Interview Questions
    1. What is a tuple?
        A tuple is an ordered, immutable collection that can store multiple values.

    2. Difference between List and Tuple?
        Lists are mutable.
        Tuples are immutable.

    3. Why are tuples faster?
        Because they are immutable, Python can optimize their storage and access.

    4. How many methods does a tuple have?
        Only two:
            count()
            index()

    5. Why would you use a tuple instead of a list?
            Use a tuple when the data should not change, such as coordinates, dates, or configuration values.
